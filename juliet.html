Scene one: Romeo is beneath Juliet's balcony shouting at her

Romeo: Oi! Juliet (No answer) I know you are up there ( a pause)

Juliet: What?

Romeo: You sent another one

Juliet: Another one what?

Romeo: Another one of your stupid love notes

Juliet: I keep telling you there is no way on Earth I would send you a love note you moron

Romeo: Well it is signed in your name

Juliet: ( A big sigh) It is my daft mother

Romeo: Your mother's name is not Juliet

Juliet: Of course it isn't you idiot. She is sending them pretending they are coming from me.

Romeo: Why?

Juliet: Because for some crazy reason she wants me to marry you

Romeo: Ha! No chance

Juliet: Exactly. I would rather marry Friar Lawrence's Donkey than you

Romeo: I don't think even the donkey would have you

Juliet: So little you know, there are men all over Verona that would give their right arm to be my husband

Romeo: Well I am not one of them

Juliet: Good

Romeo: Well, tell your mother to stop sending those stupid love notes

(Mercutio arrives)

Mercutio: Hey! Wassup bro?

Romeo: Hi mate, Juliet keeps sending me stupid love notes

Juliet: How many times do I have to tell you, I did not send them

Mercutio: Do you fancy her then?

Romeo: No I most certainly do not!

Mercutio: Hey Juliet, will you send me one ha ha

Juliet: You must be joking, you are Even less likely to get one than Romeo

( The nurse appears on the balcony with Juliet)

Nurse: What on Earth is all this din, you will wake the whole neighbourhood

Juliet: It is that idiot Romeo again, please tell him to go away Nursey

Nurse: Oh my dear, my dear, you know your mother would so like you to marry him

Romeo: No way would I marry her

Mercurio: Errr, I'm available

Juliet: I know why my mother wants me to marry him. We are the two richest family's in Verona and she thinks if we marry they will be even richer

Nurse: Oh I am sure that isn't the reason.

Romeo: Oh yes it is, and my mother thinks the same

Nurse: Juliet dear, Romeo is such a nice lad

Mercutio: And to be honest Romeo, she is a bit of alright

Juliet: I think I have made it very, very clear there is no way I am going to marry Romeo

Nurse: Don't be too hasty with that decision my dear, you do need to marry soon. After all you are nearly fifteen, you don't want to get left on the shelf

Mercutio: Are you sure you couldn't give it a go Romeo?

Romeo: Give it a go! We are talking about a lifelong marriage here, not a game of pool at the tavern

Mercutio: Only trying to help.

Juliet: Are you going to stand under my balcony all day? You are making the street look untidy

Romeo: I wouldn't be in the street if you hadn't sent those notes

Juliet: For the very last time you idiot, I did not send them

Nurse: Can you at least try to be friends? That would be a start

Juliet: I have plenty of friends and I don't need someone that makes the street look untidy as another one

Romeo: (quietly) What am I going to do Mercutio? Her mother and my mother are insisting that we marry and neither of us wants to do that

Mercutio: Hmm I can see that is a bit of a pickle

Romeo: I feel like running away. Far, far away from Verona

Mercutio: You can't do that mate. We'd be one short in the pool team.

Juliet: If you two don't soon shift I will be emptying this bucket over you – And I can tell you it is not just plain water in it

Romeo: Ok, ok we're going

Mercutio: And quickly too

Romeo: Come on Mercutio, let's go to the tavern

Mercutio: I'm right behind you my friend

Scene Two: Juliet's Bedroom

Lady Capulet: Do come away from that balcony my dear. It is very common to be howling over it.

Juliet: That awful Romeo was shouting up at me

Lady Capulet: Oh it was Romeo! That is different then. Did you invite him in for tea?

Juliet: NO! I must certainly did not

Lady Capulet: But why? He is such a charming young boy

Nurse: I think Juliet may disagree with that

Juliet: Absolutely Nursey.

Nurse: Nursey knows best

Lady Capulet: I do wish you could be friends with him Julia

Juliet: It is Juliet mother

Lady Capulet: Who's Juliet?

Juliet. (Angrily) I am!

Nurse: You called her Julia Madam, and her name is Juliet

Lady Capulet: Yes, yes of course. I meant Juliet

Juliet: And will you please stop sending those letters to Romeo pretending they are from me

Lady Capulet: (Indignant) I have sent no letters

Nurse:You did Madam, you asked me to post them for you.

Lady Capulet: (quietly) Shut up Nursey.

Juliet: And according to him they were sordid love letters

Nurse: I don't think he said anything about them being sordid my dear

Juliet: (quietly) Shut up Nursey

Lady Capulet: They were certainly not love letters, if I sent them at all, which I didn't. They were just invitations to supper.

Juliet: Can I please make this clear mother. I do not love Romeo, in fact, I don't even like him. I would rather he were never invited to lunch, dinner, supper or even breakfast. I will never, ever marry him to please you or anyone else, and if you ever send him another letter pretending it was from me I shall scream and scream and scream till I am sick

Lady Capulet: Julia my dear, grow up you are behaving like a fourteen year old

Juliet: For your information mother – I AM fourteen. And for the last time – My name is Juliet, not Julia

Nurse: She is right there Madam, it is Juliet

Lady Capulet and Juliet: (loudly) Shut up Nursey!

Scene Three. The Tavern

Friar Lawrence: Hello Lads, what can I get you?

Romeo: Friar Lawrence! What are you doing working behind the bar?

Friar Lawrence: Willie thought there were two many people already in this story so he made me double up as a barman

Mercutio: Well, it's nice to see you Friar can we have two pints of mead.

Romeo: And two packs of pork scratchings please

Friar Lawrence: I can do two pints of mead but I am afraid pork scratchings haven't been invented yet

Mercutio: I thought I saw some on the beer stall at the bazaar

Friar Lawrence: No, that is Benvolio's Dandruff

Romeo: Ok, fair enough, just the mead then

Friar Lawrence: Thou doest look troubled Romeo, what ails thee?

Romeo: What??

Mercutio: He says your face is like a donkey's rear end, wassup

Romeo: Oh, I see. My mother wants me to marry Juliet and Juliet's mother wants me to marry Juliet and neither of us wants to

Mercutio: A bit of a pickle I said

Friar Lawrence: Yes it could be. I can see why they want it though. It would make them the richest and most powerful family in Verona.

Romeo: Well I don't give two hoots about that. I'll marry who I want to marry, not who I am told to

Mercutio: It may not be that easy if the two families insist on it.

Friar Lawrence: Mercutio is right

Romeo: What am I to do then because I am definitely not going to marry Juliet

Mercutio: I think she is gorgeous

Romeo: It's not all about looks Mercutio. Juliet is a spoiled little brat who thinks of nobody but herself. She can be mean, spiteful and argumentative. I don't want to live the rest of my life with someone like that.

Mercutio: She is rich though

Friar Lawrence: Good point Mercutio

Romeo: Money, money, money. Is that all people think about?

Friar Lawrence: Most of the time, yes.

Romeo: Well I don't, there are more important things in life than money.

Mercutio: Not many though

Friar Lawrence: (thoughtfully) Hmmm, I may have an idea that will help...........

Romeo: What! What! Tell me what is it?

Friar Lawrence: Oh it's probably a bad idea, silly of me to mention it really

Mercutio: Well you have mentioned it now, so tell us what it is

Friar Lawrence: No, no it was quite a ridiculous idea really

Romeo: For goodness sake Friar, tell us what it is. I am desperate. I'd try almost anything.

Friar Lawrence: It probably wouldn't work anyway

Mercutio: Could be worth a try though.

Romeo: Yes anything, Anything is worth a try

Friar Lawrence: Ok, ok if you really want to know. This is what I suggest...............

Scene Four. The Capulets Dining Room

Lady Capulet: It would have been so nice to have had Romeo here for dinner

Juliet: No it wouldn't

Nurse: I am not sure he would have come even if he was invited

Juliet: Which if I have anything to do with it, he won't be

Lady Capulet: You should have more respect Julia for what what your father and I would like

Juliet: (almost to herself) I look quizzically around the room for this mysterious person called Julia. Perhaps she is hiding behind the curtains. Or maybe under the table. Let me take a look. Hmm no, she isn't there. (louder) Julia, Julia come out from wherever you are. Mother would like to speak to you.

Lady Capulet: What on Earth are you doing?

Juliet: I am looking for the mysterious Julia

Lady Capulet: Have you gone quite mad?

Juliet. Indeed not, I am as sane as the next person – Oh no, the next person is you mother.

Nurse: You called her Julia again madam when her name is actually Juliet.

Lady Capulet: A simple mistake. No need to write a play about it

Juliet: I sometimes wonder why you called me Juliet when you seem to much prefer calling me Julia

Lady Capulet: It wasn't my idea it was your fathers. I couldn't be bothered to think of a name for you.

Juliet: Maybe I should marry Romeo, at least he knows my name

Nurse: You have changed your tune Miss Juliet

Juliet: It was a joke Nursey

Lady Capulet: Well it wasn't a funny joke. And I am telling you now my girl your father is very insistent on you marrying Romeo. Your father will not put up with your silliness like I do. And you haven't eaten your vegetables my dear. If you don't eat your greens you won't get curly hair

Juliet: I have curly hair mother

Lady Capulet: Oh yes, so you have. I hadn't noticed. Well if you have finished eating perhaps it is your bedtime

Juliet: Yes I suppose it is

Nurse: Come on then my lovely, Lets get you ready

Juliet: Lead the way Nursey and I'll follow. Goodnight Mother

Lady Capulet: Goodnight Julia, err sorry not julia. What was it again?

Juliet: It doesn't really matter mother, goodnight

Nurse: Come on dear, up the stairs

Juliet: I am not five years old Nursey, I know how to climb the stairs

Nurse: Of course you do my darling, I just like to be sure

Juliet: Is there anything you can do Nursey to stop this marriage with Romeo

Nurse: Well my dear, it isn't really my place to interfere

Juliet: I would never ever tell mother if you help

Nurse: Well I really don't know, Maybe there is someone that can help

Juliet: Really?

Nurse: Well, I am not sure but maybe

Juliet: (excitedly) Tell me Nursey, please tell me

Nurse: Come in the bedroom dear, sit on the bed and I will explain

Juliet: Oh yes, please do

Nurse: Well let me say right away that I am sure there is nothing I can do personally

Juliet: Ok but you know someone who can?

Nurse: Maybe. But don't get too excited till I speak with him

Juliet: Ok but who is it?

Nurse: A very old friend of mine called Friar Lawrence. He is a very learned and wise man. If there is anyone in Verona that can help with this he will be the one.

Juliet: How could he help then?

Nurse: I don't really. I suppose if I did I would be as wise as he is

Juliet: Yes I suppose so. So when will you see him?

Nurse: I will sneak out tonight when you are in bed and explain the problem to him

Juliet: Oh thank you so much Nursey, come here and give me a hug

Nurse: Ohh my sweet little angel. Her's a big hug. Now you get into bed and get some sleep and I will see what I can do.

Juliet: Thank you so much, nighty night Nursey

Scene Five. The Tavern

Friar Lawrence: Now this plan may be a little dangerous but it may work

Romeo: Please, tell me more

Mercutio: I am intrigued.

Friar Lawrence: Ok, come a little coser and let me explain. You know I have a very keen interest in apothecary?

Romeo: Of course, it is common knowledge

Mercutio: An we know you cure sick people

Friar Lawrence: Very true ( A little thoughtful now) To be honest though – sometimes I seem to make them sicker

Romeo: Never mind that, what is the plan?

Friar Lawrence. Well I have this potion that would make you seem dead

Mercutio: I know you are desperate Romeo but do you really want to be dead?

Friar Lawrence: He won't be dead, it will just seem like it

Romeo: Ok, so it will seem like I am dead but how will that help?

Friar Lawrence: Your mother and father will think you are dead and they will be so distraught and upset, they will have to think about a funeral and things like that, both will be broken hearted

Mercutio: But how will that help?

Friar Lawrence: The potion lasts about four hours, then you come back to life

Romeo: Then what?

Friar Lawrence: Don't you see?

Mercutio: (puzzled) No

Romeo: I can't see it either

Friar: They will be so overjoyed that you are still alive that they will give you anything you want. And that would include letting you marry whoever you want

Romeo: (excited) Oh yes, yes, yes. I see it now. You are a genius!

Mercutio: Yes, I can see it would probably work like that

Friar Lawrence: No guarantees mind but that is the best I can offer

Romeo: I'll do it, Where's the potion?

Father Lawrence: Whoah there, slow down, this needs a bit more thought and working out properly

Nurse: Hello boys How are you

Father Lawrence: Nursey, how lovely to see you

Nurse: Lovely to see you too my dear

Mercutio: Hello Nursey

Romeo: Nice to see you here, can I get you a drink?

Nurse: That is kind of you Romeo, a small glass of porter if you would be so kind

Romeo: Small glass pf porter please Friar

Mercutio: Not often we see you here Nursey

Nurse: I am not here often it is true. I have come this evening to ask a favour of my dear friend the Friar

Friar Lawrence. Anything for you my dear, how can I help you?

Nurse: It's a little tricky really and if her Ladyship finds out about this I shall probably be sacked

Friar Lawrence. Well we don't want to see that. I know these two lads well and I know they can be discreet

Romeo: Nothing we hear will be repeated

Mercutio : And I'll probably forget it anyway

Nurse: So where do I start? I have been Juliet's nurse since she was a babe in arms and I just cannot bear to see her unhappy

Friar Lawrence: I do know how much you adore that girl.

Nurse: The problem is, her Ladyship and her husband want Juliet to marry young Romeo here

Romeo: Don't I know it

Nurse: And I just wondered with all your learning and wisdom if you could find a way perhaps to help these to youngsters out. Try to find a way for them to avoid the marriage?

Mercutio: Strange you should say that

Father Lawrence: We have just been talking about that very same situation, and I think I may have a solution. It may not work but could be worth a try

Nurse: The poor dear is desperate, I am sure she will try anything

Father Lawrence: I have special potion, called sleeper, that I want you to give to Juliet, and this is how it will work...........

Lady Capulet: Wakey wakey! Rise and shine. You should have been up hours ago (pause) Julia. Wake up my dear. You simply cannot lie there all day (Pause) Just look at all these clothes scattered all over the floor. Nurse must come and tidy up here. (pause) Julia, for goodness sake get up (Long pause) Julia, Julia wake up (in shocked voice) Oh no! Oh no no no! (shouting) My lord, my Lord come here, come here quickly our daughter is dead! My darling - errr Julia I think - is not breathing. She is dead

Scene Seven. The Tavern

Mercutio: (upset) I can't believe it. Romeo, gone!

Nurse: (in tears) My poor darling Juliet, taken from us

Mercutio: He was the best friend a fellow could ever have and now he is no more

Nurse: That poor child barely fifteen with all her life to live lying stiff and cold

Mercutio: Romeo wanted to run away, I should have let him and he would still be alive

Nurse: Juliet was so fun, so full of life now she has no life

Mercutio: Romeo Can never be replaced he was one lad in a million

Friar Lawrence: It is sad. So very, very sad

Mercutio: Sad? Sad? It is much more than sad, it is tragic. This whole thing is a tragedy

Nurse: And I came to you because I thought your were a wise and learned man

Friar Lawrence: Even wise and learned men can make mistakes. I just got sleeper and cyanide confused

Mercutio: At least they won't have to marry
